IVF and IUI are often mentioned together, but they're quite different in what they involve, who they suit, and what they cost.

## IUI, in brief

Intrauterine insemination (IUI) places prepared sperm directly into the uterus around the time of ovulation. It's less invasive and less expensive than IVF, and can suit couples with mild difficulties and at least one open fallopian tube.

## IVF, in brief

In-vitro fertilisation (IVF) involves stimulating the ovaries, collecting eggs, fertilising them in the laboratory, and transferring an embryo. It's more involved, but more effective in many situations — for example blocked tubes, significant sperm factors, or when simpler approaches haven't worked.

## Which is right for you?

That depends on your diagnosis, age, and history — not on which is "better" in general. A good consultation should explain the realistic chances of each for your situation, and be honest about when a simpler option is worth trying first.
