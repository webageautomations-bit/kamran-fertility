A semen analysis is one of the most useful early tests in fertility, and one of the most misunderstood. A single result is a snapshot, not a verdict.

## What it measures

- **Count** — how many sperm are present
- **Motility** — how well they move
- **Morphology** — their size and shape
- **Volume** and other basic parameters

## Why one result isn't the whole story

Sperm production varies, and results can be affected by recent illness, fever, or even the time since the last sample. A below-range result is often repeated after a few weeks before any conclusions are drawn.

## What happens next

If a result is outside the usual range, the next step is a calm conversation — not alarm. Many factors are modifiable, and many couples conceive with support. We explain what the numbers mean for you, specifically.
